Privacy Policy for SecurePass

Effective Date: March 25, 2026

1. Zero-Knowledge Architecture & Complete Offline Isolation

SecurePass is architected as a strict zero-knowledge, completely offline password vault. We do not own servers, we do not host a cloud, and the SecurePass application strictly strips all android.permission.INTERNET capabilities at the compiled operating system kernel level. It is mathematically and physically impossible for SecurePass to transmit, alter, or view your network settings or leak your data to the internet.

2. Data Collection and Usage

SecurePass does not collect, harvest, transmit, or share any personal data, passwords, usernames, analytics, or crash reports. Your data remains 100% encrypted on your local physical device utilizing Argon2id cryptographic key derivation paired with AES-GCM encryption.

3. Master Password Governance

We do not know, store, or transmit your Master Password. You are solely responsible for memorizing it. Because of our strict zero-knowledge architecture, if you forget your Master Password, your encrypted vault cannot be recovered by our developers under any circumstances.

4. External Backups

You have the explicit option to export a .pvault backup file. This file remains heavily encrypted with dynamic 16-byte cryptographic salts. The routing of this file to external platforms (such as Google Drive or Email) is entirely initiated and controlled by you via the Android operating system Share protocol.

5. Contact Us

If you have any questions entirely regarding this Privacy Policy, please contact the developer at: support@ipreu.co.in.
