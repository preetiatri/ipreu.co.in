SecurePassword Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 2026

1. Introduction

SecurePassword is a zero-knowledge password manager designed to keep your digital credentials safe and private. This Privacy Policy outlines our commitment to high-security, local-only data management.

2. Zero-Knowledge Architecture

No Access to Your Data: We do not have access to your passwords. All encryption and decryption happen locally on your device. Your master password is never sent to our servers, and we never have the key to unlock your digital vault.

Encryption Standards: We use industry-standard AES-256 encryption to ensure your data remains unreadable even if the physical device is compromised, provided you use a strong master password.

3. Information Collection

Zero Personal Data: SecurePassword does not collect your name, email, or any other personal identifier. We do not track your vault's contents or your usage patterns.

4. Permissions and Privacy

The app only requests permissions necessary for its core function (e.g., local storage access to save the encrypted vault). We do not request access to your contacts, camera, location, or browsing history.

5. Data Retention and Deletion

You have absolute control over your data. Deleting the vault file from your device or unisntalling the app will permanently remove all stored credentials. We maintain no backups of your data.

6. Third-Party Services

SecurePassword contains no third-party tracking, analytics, or advertising SDKs. Your passwords and security are our only priority.

7. Children's Privacy

SecurePassword is not intended for use by individuals under the age of 13.

8. Changes to This Policy

Any revisions to this privacy policy will be posted on this page with an updated revision date.
